# Little Bighorn 1876 scenario

This scenario is transcribed from:

- `docs/TRANSCRIPTION-DECISIONS.md` (authoritative on conflicts)
- `docs/research/little-bighorn-research.md` (historical tables A–K)

The bibliography keys in `scenario.json` cover Gray (1991), Fox (1993), Michno,
Scott (1987), Hedren (1973), RCOI testimony, Terry's 1876 order, Reno and Benteen
reports, Godfrey, NPS, HMDB, and the secondary sources catalogued by the research
dataset. `WEB_MISC` remains explicitly weak and includes forum, relay, and other
low-tier sources; it is not treated as upgraded evidence.

## Known weaknesses

The research could not establish the following at MEDIUM confidence or better:

- The precise Crow's Nest coordinate. The real Wolf Mountains overlook is on
  private land; the published visitor-site marker is a decoy and is not used.
- Minute-by-minute routes and timing for Custer's five companies after about 15:30.
  These depend on Native eyewitness accounts and archaeology because no white
  witness survived.
- Per-circle populations and fighting-strength contributions. Ordering and some
  lodge estimates are firmer than population shares.
- Native casualties, for which published figures range from roughly 36 to 300.
- Exact 1876 village geometry and pony-herd size.

Rows resting on a single source include the Minneconjou lodge estimate, Hedren's
extraction-failure rate (with Scott offering a different sample), several leader-axis
attributions, Curley's visibility, and the 62 Henry / 27 Sharps Custer-field breakdown.

The three weakest simulation areas are:

1. The Custer field after Medicine Tail Coulee: Fox, Michno, and traditional models
   imply materially different tracks and timings and must remain separate branches.
2. The Native order of battle: warrior count and firearm fractions are ranges, and
   combat resolution is sensitive to the selected values.
3. 1876 terrain: river migration and changed timber mean modern DEM results need the
   1891 channel correction before line-of-sight and fording results are trustworthy.

All unresolved transcription/contract issues are marked `TODO-AMBIGUOUS` in record
provenance and itemized in the root `codex-report.md`.
