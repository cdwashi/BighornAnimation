# Little Bighorn (25 June 1876): Simulation-Ready Historical Dataset

**Scope note:** This is a data-compilation deliverable, not a narrative. Times are anchored to John S. Gray's local-sun chronology (*Custer's Last Campaign*, 1991). Positions are WGS84 decimal degrees. Disputed quantities are given low/best/high and disputed interpretations are reported as labeled rows, never blended. Confidence scale: **HIGH** (physical archaeology or multiple independent primary accounts) / **MEDIUM** (single credible primary account or strong scholarly consensus) / **LOW** (inference/reconstruction) / **DISPUTED** (competing published interpretations — each shown separately).

## Conventions & clock offsets
Gray reconstructs events in **local apparent (sun) time** on the battlefield. Contemporary witnesses used at least three inconsistent clocks (Chicago railroad time; personal watches set at Fort Lincoln; rough estimates). Officer watch-times in the Reno Court of Inquiry (RCOI) and Godfrey's account typically run **~45–70 minutes ahead** of Gray's sun time in the early afternoon (e.g., Godfrey's "about one o'clock" for the division corresponds to Gray's ~12:00 sun time). Curley's report that the Custer fight ran "10 o'clock to noon" reflects his own uncalibrated estimate and is treated as unreliable for absolute timing. Every timeline row is Gray sun time unless flagged.

## F. Disputed Interpretations (alternatives reported, never blended)

**F1. Medicine Tail Coulee — feint vs. serious crossing**
| Interpretation | Principal proponents | Supporting evidence | Implication |
|---|---|---|---|
| Feint / reconnaissance, light resistance | Gray; Fox; GCSU/Corinthian review (only ~17 items/very few cases at ford) | Sparse archaeology at ford; Cooke and staff died on Last Stand Hill (not at the ford) | E,F probe then withdraw via Deep Coulee to Calhoun/Custer Ridge |
| Serious attempted crossing repulsed | Utley (older view); Benteen/Reno as post-battle observers | "hammer-and-anvil" logic; troopers' belief | Custer possibly checked at the river |
Companies involved: **E & F (Yates, left wing)** approached the ford; **C, I, L (Keogh, right wing)** held the ridges.

**F2. Custer's wing structure after MTC**
Left wing **E, F** (Yates) toward Ford B/D; right wing **C, I, L** (Keogh) to Calhoun/Battle Ridge. Fox (citing the period cavalry manual) argues wings need not be equal, countering the theory that C Company split to balance strength. DISPUTED whether C was divided.

**F3. Sequence/character of the collapse**
| Model | Proponent | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| "Tactical disintegration" — no last stand; rapid collapse amid panic | Fox (1993) | Sparse, scattered cartridge clusters; few defensive concentrations |
| Organized resistance / genuine last stand; longer phase | Traditional accounts; some Michno points | Calhoun Hill skirmish line; horse-carcass breastworks on LSH |
Duration of final phase: "a matter of minutes" up to ~half an hour (multiple accounts).

**F4. Deep Ravine / missing bodies**
~28 bodies (mostly Company E) reported in the ravine by the 1876 burial detail; never located by 1984/85/1989 archaeology (1995–96 remote sensing found soil anomalies). Fox: they died in the ravine and remain buried under erosional infill. Michno: the South Skirmish Line markers are valid indicators. Scott: geomorphological processes over 108+ years rendered remains unrecoverable. DISPUTED.

**F5. Reno leaving the timber**
Orderly withdrawal vs. panic rout. Most RCOI officers defended Reno (Moylan, Hare, DeRudio "admired his conduct"); Herendeen and Girard were critical. Bloody Knife's death beside Reno is cited as the demoralizing trigger. Position tenability also disputed (Girard: could have held; others: would have been "shut in"). DISPUTED.

**F6. Benteen's pace returning with the packs**
Reasonable (RCOI did not condemn him) vs. dilatory ("Benteen failed Custer," Wert; "dawdling," common criticism). DISPUTED — RCOI treatment more lenient than later scholarship.

**F7. Village size / degree of outnumbering**
Army estimate ~800 warriors vs. reality ~1,500–2,000; village 949–996 lodges / ~7,000 people. Benteen later inflated the warrior count to 8,000–9,000 (self-serving). Best working figure: **1,500–2,000 warriors**; the tradition of overwhelming odds is directionally correct but the extreme numbers are not supported.

**F8. Curley's reliability**
Gray rehabilitates Curley, arguing contradictions stem from questioners' flawed assumptions, not Curley. Others dismiss his accounts. Consensus: he left before the end and may have observed part of the fight from a distance (~1.5 mi). DISPUTED.

**F9. "Custer shot early at the ford"**
White Cow Bull claimed to have shot a buckskin-clad leader off his horse at the river; **no other Indian account corroborates**. Fox/GCSU reject it (Custer's adjutant and staff died on LSH; his wounds are consistent with death there). LOW.

**F10. Timing offsets between chronologies**
Gray (local sun time) vs. Michno (*Lakota Noon*, compresses the afternoon and starts the Custer fight relatively early) vs. Curley (uncalibrated "10:00–noon"). Watch-based officer testimony (RCOI, Godfrey) runs ~45–70 min ahead of Gray. Custer-fight window is most defensibly ~16:00–17:00 sun time. All chronologies agree Reno-Benteen did **not** know Custer's fate until 27 June.

## K. Casualties and Outcome

**7th Cavalry (Wikipedia, verbatim):** 52% casualties — **16 officers and 242 troopers killed or died of wounds; 1 officer and 51 troopers wounded.** All ~210 men of Custer's five companies were killed. Total dead including scouts and civilians ≈ **268**: the July 1881 granite monument was inscribed with **263 names**; five enlisted men who died of wounds after evacuation were not included — with them the total rises to 268 (HISTORY.com).

| Custer's five companies (C,E,F,I,L) | ~210 | Annihilated |
| Reno/Benteen (A,G,M,H,D,K,B) | ~53 killed, ~52 wounded | Valley + hilltop |

**Officers killed (named):** G.A. Custer; Capt. Tom Custer (C); Capt. George Yates (F); Capt. Myles Keogh (I); 1Lt James Calhoun (L); 1Lt Algernon Smith (E); 1Lt James Porter (I); 2Lt William Reily (F); 2Lt James Sturgis (E); 2Lt Henry Harrington (C); 2Lt John Crittenden (L, 20th Inf); 1Lt W.W. **Horses:** Nearly all of Custer's mounts were lost; **Comanche** (Keogh's horse) was the sole army animal found alive on the Custer field.

**Ammunition/expenditure evidence:** 2,361 cartridges, cases, and bullets recovered battlefield-wide; **min. 371 individual firearms**, 45 types. **Medals of Honor:** 24 awarded for the hilltop fight (per National Parks Traveler): 15 to troopers who volunteered to fetch river water under fire, plus 4 sharpshooters who provided covering fire (a 16th water carrier, later leg-amputated, received none).

## Gaps and Reliability Report

**(1) Could NOT be established at MEDIUM confidence or better:**
- Precise coordinates of the Crow's Nest (actual Wolf Mountains overlook is on private land; only the interpretive sign at Reno-Benteen has a published coordinate — a decoy that must not be used).
- Minute-by-minute routes and timing of Custer's five companies after ~15:30 — this rests **entirely** on Native eyewitness testimony (Michno, Hardorff, Marquis) plus archaeology; no surviving white witness.
- Per-circle populations and each circle's fighting-strength contribution (only ordering and lodge estimates are firm).
- The true Native casualty figure (competing figures span ~36 to ~300; each is provenance-tagged above).
- Exact 1876 village geometry (length/width) and pony-herd size.

**(2) Rows resting on a single source:**
- Minneconjou ~150 lodges (Gray).
- Extraction-failure rate <0.35% (Hedren 1973; Scott corroborates with a different sample).
- Several leader-axis attributions (Crow King's ~80 warriors; Lame White Man's death circumstances).
- Curley's visibility of the Custer fight.
- The "62 Henry / 27 Sharps on Custer's field" breakdown (Scott/Fox via one secondary relay).

**(3) Three areas where the dataset is weakest for simulation:**
- **(a) The Custer field after Medicine Tail Coulee** — the Fox "tactical disintegration," Michno, and traditional "organized last stand" models yield materially different unit tracks and timings; the scenario must implement these as switchable branches, not a single path.
- **(b) Native order of battle** — warrior count (700–2,500 published) and weapons fractions (1/3 to 1/2 armed) are ranges, not points; combat resolution is highly sensitive to which value is chosen.
- **(c) 1876 terrain vs. modern** — the river channel has migrated measurably (esp. near Ford B and the Garryowen loop) and timber extent has changed, so any modern-DEM-based line-of-sight/fording model must be corrected against the 1891 channel overlay before it is trustworthy.
